Ziwei Zhu     竺紫威

Hi, I am a quantitative researcher at Radix Trading. I was an assistant professor of statistics at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor (UMich) from 2019 to 2022. Prior to UMich, I was a research associate at the Statistical Laboratory at the Univeristy of Cambridge, hosted by Professor Richard J. Samworth. I obtained my Ph.D. degree at the Department of Operations Research and Financial Engineering (ORFE) at Princeton University, advised by Professor Jianqing Fan. I did my undergraduate study at the Special Class of Gifted Young at the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC). My research interests are federated / distributed statistical learning, high-dimensional statistics, robust statistics and missing data.
 My academic research focus is on best subset selection and communication-efficient statistical learning algorithms. I appreciate the funding support from NSF DMS 2015366, for which I served as the PI.

  • A new arXiv release (joint work with Yumeng Wang): ReBoot: Distributed statistical learning via refitting Bootstrap samples. This work studies a one-shot distributed learning algorithm based on refitting Bootstrap samples drawn from multiple local models, which we refer to ReBoot. ReBoot accommodates complicated models such as neural nets and has provably less systematic bias than model parameter averaging and its variants. (Jul, 2022)
